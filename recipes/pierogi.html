Pierogi

Pierogi are a form of dumpling with an incredible degree of versatility. They can be stuffed with all manner of ingredients and then served on their own, embellished with sauce, in a soup... the limit is the chef's imagination. This recipe will create a basic pierogi stuffed with mashed potato and cheese.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups unbleached all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 8 tbsp butter (have half ready at room temperature for the dough)
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes (warm)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar
  • 2 large shallots (diced) OR one medium onion (sliced)

Steps

  1. We start with the dough. Mix the flour and salt. Add egg and combine until clumpy.
  2. Work in 4 tbsp of softened butter and the sour cream until the dough forms a rough ball.
  3. Knead and fold the dough with your fingertips. Knead until the dough is moist, but not as sticky.
  4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 to 60 minutes; it will keep for 48 hours if delayed preparation is desired.
  5. Make the filling by combining the potato and cheese. Mash them together until the cheese is melted and the mixture is cool. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Roll half the dough out to a thickness of 1/8". Cut out 2" circles of dough with a cutter. Repeat with the remaining dough.
  7. Place 1 1/2 tsp of filling at the center of each dough circle.
  8. Fold each circle and pinch the edges to form a pocket around the filling. Further seal the edge by pressing down with the tines of a fork.
  9. Save the pierogis for later, if desired. They can be frozen for 4 weeks or refrigerated overnight.
  10. Boil the pierogi in a pot of salted water. Add enough to give each one space to float without sticking to others.
  11. Remove and drain the pierogi when they float.
  12. In a large skillet, saute your chosen allium in the remaining butter. Once they begin to brown, add the pierogi.
  13. Serve hot with any other desired embellishments.
Recipe source from King Arthur Baking
